Modern Foreign Research in Creativity:
The Social Psychological Aspect

N.V. MESHKOVA,
PhD in psychology, associate professor at the Chair of Theoretical Foundations

of Social Psychology, Moscow State University of Psychology and Education

The paper provides a review of modern foreign studies on social psychological aspects of
creativity. It focuses on the explorations of environmental (e.g. family, culture, education
and workplace environments) and situational factors that help unleash creative potential of
an individual and discusses cultural aspects of concepts of creativity. Special attention is
paid to the studies that explore the relationship between situations, individual differences
and creativity. As it is revealed, negative situational factors (such as threats, time limits, fear
of death and nostalgia) may actually have a positive impact on creativity. Also, the paper
highlights the difficulties concerning the assessment of creative potential as well as of cre2
ative outcome.

Keywords: creativity, creative potential, development of creativity, organized creativity,
social psychology of creativity, evaluation of creative product, creativity assessment.
