Key issues of determining the settings of coordinates for characteristic points  
of land boundaries using the photogrammetric method

a.M. tararin
Moscow state university of Geodesy and Cartography, Moscow, russia

tammiigaik@gmail.com
received december 6, 2018 revised February 19, 2019 accepted april 4, 2019
Keywords: boundaries of land, determination of the coordinates, photogrammetric method, settings for characteristic points.

Summary. The Unified State Register of Immovable Property is lacking information about the boundaries of 
29 million land plots. Some parcels have intersections, shifts and other inconsistencies in the boundaries. New 
technological solutions are required to solve the logjam of issues. As a solution, it is proposed to determine 
the location of boundaries of land plots using the photogrammetric method. It is possible to define the settings 
of coordinates for characteristic points using both stereoscopic models and monocular measurements over 
a variety of aerial photographs. Furthermore, orthophotoplans are to be used to fix and display the obtained 
measurements. Existing regulations for aerial photography and photogrammetric works are to be updated. That 
will entail a change in requirements to the photogrammetric method employed in cadastral works. It would 
be reasonable to develop guidelines for the shared use of the photogrammetric method, analytical (graphic) 
method and geodetic crossbearing when performing cadastral works, including integrated cadastral works. 
The usage of the Earth's remote sensing data in surveying provides additional security to land titles due to the 
visibility of displayed boundaries of land plots with reference to the terrain objects identified by proprietors.
