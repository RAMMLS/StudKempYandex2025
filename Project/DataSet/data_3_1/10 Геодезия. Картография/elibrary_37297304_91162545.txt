Keywords: ablation, GIS, glacier, remote sensing, unmanned aircraft systems 

Summary. Observations on the glaciers of the Mongolian Altai mountains are necessary to detect the changing 
climate pattern in this region. There are more than 1000 glaciers and over the past decade they have undergone 
significant changes in glacial length and area. The dynamics of glaciers is one of the most important factors of 
freshwater sources for local population. Two glaciers on the Tavan Bogd mountains, Mongolian Altai, namely 
Potanin and Alexandra glaciers, have been under detailed observation for about 10 years. Potanin glacier is 
one of the best-studied and biggest glaciers on this territory that is why it perfectly fits for trying out a new 
methodology. In this study, the technology of unmanned aircraft systems deployment above valley glaciers 
and the calculation of height changes during the ablation season are presented. The results of the expedition to 
Potanin and Alexandra glaciers in 2015 are shown. The expedition was divided into two parts: the beginning 
and the end of the estimated ablation season (beginning of June – end of August). This allowed researchers 
to obtain two digital surface models and orthomosaics; all the necessary characteristics of the glaciers were 
calculated in this way. The main idea was to perform such research without geodetic ground control points 
which were unnecessary because a GNSS receiver of a geodesic class had been installed in the unmanned 
aircraft systems.
