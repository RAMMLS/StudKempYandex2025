case of the Wrangel and Kunashir islands

Keywords: Kunashir island, Landsat, land surface temperature, thermal infrared images, Wrangel island.

Summary. In present, many remote areas are characterized by low density of meteorological stations, which 
reduces the accuracy of synoptic forecasts for territories, remote from the weather stations. In this regard, the 
use of satellite thermal infrared images for simulation of certain climatic parameters considered by the authors 
as a promising focus area of aerospace methods of geographical research. The article presents the results 
of comparing the land surface temperature values calculated using thermal infrared images from satellites 
Landsat series and the results of ground measurements of the surface atmosphere layer temperature. The study 
area — Wrangel and Kunashir islands — characterized by different landscape conditions (on Wrangel island 
tundra and barrens are presented, on Kunashir island — coniferous and mixed forests), in addition, on these 
islands state natural reserves «Wrangel Island» and «Kurilsky» are located, where the networks of ground 
observation of climatic parameters are organized. Calculation of the land surface temperature values held by 
a standard single-channel method developed by NASA specialists. For the considered seasons the analyzed 
indicators (land surface temperature values calculated using thermal infrared satellite images and surface 
atmosphere layer temperature values obtained by ground observations) are characterized by a pronounced 
linear relationship with a high correlation coefficient. Basing on the scatterplots made on these two indicators, 
we held the trend lines and their equations used then for the calculation of the atmosphere surface layer 
temperature fields. The resulting calculated atmosphere surface layer temperature values are compared with 
the measured, the standard deviation was about 1°C.
